Research

My projects utilize mouse and cell culture models to understand retinal disease mechanisms. Often this involves a combination of high-resolution imaging, protein analysis, in vivo testing, and more recently, DNA sequencing. By focusing on proteins associated with human disease, the research addresses questions such as:

  • What functions does this protein have in healthy photoreceptor cells?
  • How do disease-causing mutations disrupt these functions and lead to photoreceptor cell death?
  • How can we prevent or delay retinal degeneration using gene therapy or other interventions?

My current focus is on the visual system and diseases such as retinitis pigmentosa. Neuromuscular and neurodegenerative disease mechanisms remain a long-standing interest. In previous labs, I worked on proteins associated with muscular dystrophy and related conditions like Walker-Warburg syndrome and Muscle-Eye-Brain disease.
